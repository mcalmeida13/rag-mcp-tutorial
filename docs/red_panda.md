# Red Panda

The red panda (Ailurus fulgens) is a small mammal native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Despite its name, it is not closely related to the giant panda; genetic studies place it in its own family, Ailuridae, making it the sole living member of that lineage.

Red pandas are about the size of a domestic cat, with rust-colored fur, a long bushy tail with alternating light and dark rings, and a mask-like facial pattern. The thick tail helps them balance while climbing and wraps around the body for warmth during cold mountain nights.

They are mostly solitary and crepuscular, meaning they are most active at dawn and dusk. Their diet consists primarily of bamboo, though they also eat fruit, acorns, roots, and occasionally eggs or small insects. Red pandas have a "false thumb," an extended wrist bone that helps them grip bamboo stalks, a feature they share convergently with giant pandas despite being unrelated.

Red pandas are classified as endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and a shrinking, fragmented population across their native range.
